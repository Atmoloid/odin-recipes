Hamburger

The hamburger has ancient roots in the dishes of the Tartars, but its popularity exploded in Germany, from where German emigrants brought it to the United States. Here, it transformed into the fast food icon we know today, conquering the world with its simplicity and versatility.

Ingredients
    Meat,Tomato,lettuce,cheese,buns
Steps
  • Prepare the patty: Combine ground beef, salt, and pepper in a bowl. Mix gently until just combined. Form the mixture into 4-ounce patties. Make a small indentation in the center of each patty to prevent it from puffing up during cooking.
  • Cook the patty: Heat a cast iron skillet or grill to medium-high heat. Add a little oil to the skillet. Cook the patties for 4-5 minutes per side, or until cooked to your desired level of doneness.
  • Assemble the burger: Toast your buns. Place a cooked patty on each bottom bun. Add your favorite toppings, such as lettuce, tomato, onion, cheese, and condiments. Top with the bun top.
