Gyoza

Gyoza are delicious Japanese dumplings filled with savory ingredients like pork, cabbage, and garlic. They're typically pan-fried until crispy on one side and served with a dipping sauce.

Ingredients
    Ground pork or a combination of pork and shrimp,Cabbage, finely chopped,Garlic,minced,Ginger, grated,Scallions, finely chopped,Sesame oil,Soy sauce,Rice vinegar,Salt and pepper Â 
Steps
  • Combine ingredients: In a large bowl, mix together the ground pork (or pork and shrimp), finely chopped cabbage, minced garlic, grated ginger, finely chopped scallions, sesame oil, soy sauce, rice vinegar, salt, and pepper. Chill the filling: Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  • Prepare the wrappers: Place the wonton wrappers on a lightly floured surface. Add filling: Place a spoonful of the chilled filling in the center of each wrapper. Fold and seal: Fold the wrapper over the filling, then fold the sides in and pinch the edges firmly to seal.
  • Heat a pan: Heat a skillet or frying pan over medium-high heat. Cook the gyoza: Add a layer of gyoza to the pan, making sure they don't overlap. Cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the bottoms are golden brown. Add water: Pour a small amount of water into the pan, just enough to cover the bottoms of the gyoza. Cover the pan and cook for another 2-3 minutes, or until the water has evaporated and the gyoza are cooked through. Serve: Remove the gyoza from the pan and serve immediately with your favorite dipping sauce.
